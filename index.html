Our Dates

Jan 16–30, 2027

We've nailed the sweet spot. The second half of January is peak powder season — Niseko averages ~100cm of snowfall per week, with the coldest temperatures (-9°C avg) and deepest powder of the year. Japanese holidays ended Jan 3, Australian holidays are winding down, and Chinese New Year doesn't hit until Feb 6. This gives us two full weeks of the best snow-to-crowd ratio of the entire season. Snow depth will be 250–350cm and building. Expect waist-deep powder days.

Niseko

Four interconnected resorts on one mountain, legendary powder, and the best après scene in Japan.

The most famous powder destination in Japan. Four interconnected resorts — Grand Hirafu, Hanazono, Niseko Village, and Annupuri — offer huge terrain variety from groomers to steep backcountry gates. English-friendly with an excellent international food and nightlife scene in Hirafu village.

The Four Resorts

  • Grand Hirafu — Largest area, best nightlife, most runs. The main base.
  • Hanazono — Tree runs, powder bowls, family-friendly. Quieter.
  • Niseko Village — Steeper terrain, ski-in/ski-out hotels, good tree skiing.
  • Annupuri — Gentler slopes, quieter, great for intermediates and Japanese-style lodges.

Why Niseko

  • Legendary consistent dry powder — 15m+ annually
  • Night skiing until 8:30pm at Grand Hirafu
  • Great nightlife, restaurants, and bars in Hirafu
  • Easy for English speakers — menus, signs, staff
  • Backcountry gates for off-piste access
  • Views of Mt. Yotei (the "Mt. Fuji of Hokkaido")

Lift Pass

Niseko United All Mountain Pass: ~¥8,100/day (~£41) · 8-day pass ~¥56,000 (~£285) · 14-day pass ~¥84,000 (~£427)

Getting There

London to Niseko via New Chitose Airport (CTS), Sapporo.

Flights — London to Sapporo

London (LHR) → Tokyo (HND) → New Chitose (CTS)

No direct flights exist — all routes require at least one connection. The best option is via Tokyo Haneda on JAL or ANA, then a short 1.5hr domestic hop to CTS.

Best Airlines

  • JAL (Japan Airlines) — LHR–HND daily, most CTS connections (~176/week). Gold standard for this route. Ski bags fly free.
  • ANA — Similar routing via HND/NRT, excellent service, ~32 connecting flights/week to CTS.
  • Finnair — LHR–Helsinki–Tokyo–CTS. Worth checking if they reinstate their direct Helsinki–CTS seasonal route for 26/27.
  • BA + JAL — BA to Tokyo, then JAL domestic. Use Avios for the long-haul leg (~34,000–40,000 Avios return in economy).

Flights: ~£1,000–1,300 return (economy, JAL/ANA, booked 4-6 months ahead)

CTS to Niseko — Transfers

New Chitose (CTS) → Niseko (2–3 hrs)

Shared Coach (Best Value)

Hokkaido Resort Liner — Direct to Hirafu/Niseko Village/Annupuri. ~¥6,000 one-way (~£30). Must book 9+ days ahead via access-n.jp. Daily departures Dec–Mar.

Private Transfer (Best for Groups)

Wheels Hokkaido — 8-seater Hiace van: ~¥81,000 one-way (~£52/person for 8). Door-to-door, handles all the ski bags. Book via wheelshokkaido.com.

SkyExpress / Explore Niseko — Alternative private transfer operators with similar pricing.

Train (Not Recommended)

CTS → Sapporo → Otaru → Kutchan. 3+ hours, two changes, nightmare with ski bags. Avoid.

Booking Tips

  • Book flights 4–6 months ahead (by July–Aug 2026) for best prices
  • For 10+ passengers, call JAL/ANA group desks directly — hold seats with a deposit, flexible name changes
  • JAL and ANA count ski bags as standard luggage — confirm allowances when booking
  • Our return date (Jan 30) is just before Chinese New Year (Feb 6) — airports will be getting busier
  • Book bus/private transfers as soon as flights are confirmed — popular times fill up weeks ahead
  • Consider a Wise or Revolut card for the best exchange rates in Japan

Where to Stay

Niseko has everything from luxury chalets to traditional ryokans and budget hostels. Here are real options across the spectrum for a group of 6–8, with approximate per-person-per-night costs.

Western-Style Chalet

Skye Niseko — 4 Bed Suite ~£55–75 pp/night

Premium ski-in/ski-out condo hotel at the base of Grand Hirafu. 4-bedroom corner suite with full hotel amenities, en-suite bathrooms, ski valet, and onsen. skyeniseko.com

Traditional Ryokan

Zaborin — Hanazono ~£380+ pp/night

15 private villas in a forested setting, each with its own indoor and outdoor private onsen. Award-winning kaiseki dinner and breakfast included. Tattoo-friendly. Best as a 1–2 night splurge. zaborin.com

Budget Lodge

Moiwa Lodge — Moiwa ~£30–50 pp/night

Slopeside at Moiwa ski area with free 3-min shuttle to Annupuri. Self-contained cottages sleeping 8 available. Free breakfast. Some of the nicest dorms on any ski field. moiwalodge.com

Mid-Range Japanese

Annupuri Lodge — Annupuri ~£60–90 pp/night

8 individually-designed rooms, 2 min walk to Annupuri gondola. Breakfast included. On-site Luckyfingers bar serving izakaya-style food. annupurilodge.com

Booking Tips

  • Book accommodation 3–6 months ahead — popular spots sell out fast for January
  • Annupuri has the best concentration of Japanese-character properties and is quieter and cheaper than Hirafu
  • A chalet/lodge with a drying room is a game-changer for ski gear
  • Many Japanese lodges include dinner and breakfast — factor this into the price comparison
  • Consider splitting the trip: budget hostel for most nights + 1–2 nights in a ryokan for the full experience
  • Look on Booking.com, Airbnb, and the resort-specific booking sites listed above

Sample 14-Day Itinerary

Two weeks of powder, onsens, and izakayas. Here's how to pace it without burning out.

Day 1

Fri Jan 16 — Arrive in Niseko

Fly into New Chitose Airport, transfer to Niseko (2.5hrs). Check in, grab gear rentals, get your bearings around Hirafu. Welcome ramen and beers.

Day 2

Sat Jan 17 — First Day on Snow

Ease into it — explore Grand Hirafu, find your legs, scope out the mountain. Après at the base, then dinner in Hirafu village.

Day 3

Sun Jan 18 — Full Send

First lifts to last lifts. Hit the steeps, find the powder stashes, explore all four resorts. Night skiing at Grand Hirafu until 8:30pm.

Day 4

Mon Jan 19 — Ski + Onsen

Morning ski session, then afternoon at Goshiki Onsen (outdoor rotenburo with mountain views, ~¥800). Evening yakitori at Bang Bang in Hirafu.

Day 5

Tue Jan 20 — Powder Day

If the snow gods deliver, this is your day. Early start, fresh tracks, waist-deep turns in the Hanazono trees. This is why you came to Japan.

Day 6

Wed Jan 21 — Rest Day: Distillery + Otaru

Morning tour at Niseko Distillery (~¥2,000, whisky + gin tasting). Then drive to Otaru — canal town with incredible seafood and sushi. Back for evening drinks at Bar Gyu+.

Day 7

Thu Jan 22 — Back on the Mountain

Refreshed legs, back on snow. Explore Niseko Village and Annupuri sides. Try Jingisukan (Hokkaido lamb BBQ) at Rin for dinner.

Day 8

Fri Jan 23 — Full Day + Night Out

Another big ski day. Evening: cocktails at Bar Gyu+ (hidden fridge-door entrance), then Powder Room nightclub or Tamashii for the late session.

Day 9

Sat Jan 24 — Snowmobiles + Ski

Morning snowmobile tour through the forests (~¥10,000–15,000, guided 60 min). Afternoon ski session. Evening onsen at Niseko Grand Hotel — huge mixed outdoor rotenburo in the snow (~¥1,500).

Day 10

Sun Jan 25 — Chilled Ski Day

Sleep in, cruise the groomers, enjoy the mountain at your own pace. Lunch on-mountain. Evening ramen at Tozanken followed by Bar Haku for cocktails.

Day 11

Mon Jan 26 — Rest Day: Yoichi + Otaru

Day trip to Nikka Whisky Yoichi Distillery (free tour, paid tastings). Stop at Otaru on the way back for canal walk and seafood dinner.

Day 12

Tue Jan 27 — Powder Chase

Full day on snow — this deep into January, the snowpack is massive (300cm+). Find the untracked lines. Night skiing if legs allow.

Day 13

Wed Jan 28 — Last Full Ski Day

Make it count. Revisit your favourite runs, take photos and videos, soak it all in. Big farewell izakaya dinner with the boys.

Day 14

Thu Jan 29 — Morning Ski + Depart

Quick morning laps if time allows, then transfer to CTS for evening flights home. Or add a night in Sapporo — ramen alley, Susukino bars, and Sapporo Beer Museum.

Beyond the Slopes

Niseko isn't just about skiing. Onsens, whisky, world-class food, and day trips to some of Hokkaido's best towns.

♨️

Onsens

Niseko sits on volcanic ground with some of Japan's best hot springs. Nothing beats soaking in an outdoor rotenburo surrounded by falling snow after a day on the mountain.

  • Goshiki Onsen — Indoor + outdoor, rich mineral water, mountain views. ~¥800. Near Annupuri.
  • Niseko Grand Hotel — Massive mixed-gender outdoor rotenburo (bathing wear provided). ~¥1,500. Spectacular in a snowstorm.
  • Kanronomori — Private 50-min sessions for ~¥3,000. Great for a group booking.
  • One Niseko — Indoor + outdoor at Moiwa. ~¥950 including towels.
  • Yu Meguri Pass — Visit 3 onsens for ~¥2,100. Great for onsen-hopping.

Etiquette: onsens are nude, no tattoo cover-ups needed at some (check ahead). Wash thoroughly before entering. No phones in the bathing area.

🥃

Whisky & Drinks

Hokkaido is home to some of Japan's finest distilleries, and Niseko now has its own.

  • Niseko Distillery — Founded by century-old sake brewery Hakkaisan. Tours at 10:00 and 15:00 daily, ~¥2,000. Whisky and craft gin using snowmelt water. Book at niseko-distillery.com.
  • Nikka Whisky Yoichi — 1hr from Niseko. Free tours (book ahead). Paid tastings including Yoichi Single Cask. Restaurant serving Scottish dishes paired with whisky. Combine with an Otaru stop.
🍜

Food

Hokkaido has some of the best food in Japan — fresh seafood, miso ramen, lamb BBQ, and farm produce.

  • Bang Bang — Go-to yakitori izakaya. Charcoal-grilled skewers. Book ahead.
  • Rin — Jingisukan (Hokkaido lamb BBQ) with local vegetables. Uniquely Hokkaido.
  • Tozanken — No-reservations ramen shop. Asahikawa-style pork broth. ~£4–6.
  • AFURI — Yuzu-infused ramen in a stylish setting. Originally from Tokyo.
  • Sushi Shin — At Sansui Niseko. Branch of Sapporo's Michelin 3-star Sushi Miyakawa. Splurge dinner.
  • Ryunabe — Premium Chinese hot pot with Hokkaido seafood. Great for groups.
  • Milk Kobo — Famous soft serve, choux creme, and cheese tarts. Essential stop.
🎿

Other Winter Activities

  • Snowmobiling — Guided 60-min tours through forests with Mt. Yotei views. ~¥10,000–15,000. Via Hanazono or Explore Niseko.
  • Snowshoeing — Guided treks through birch forests. NOASC runs private group tours (~¥29,500 for 1–4 people).
  • Snow Tubing — Hanazono snow tube park with conveyor lift. ~¥2,500/hr. Night sessions available.
  • Sushi-Making Class — Learn to make 13 types of sushi + tamagoyaki. Bookable through local activity desks or ByFood.
🏙️

Day Trips

  • Otaru (1.5–2hrs) — Beautiful Meiji-era canal town. Stunning in winter snow. Outstanding sushi restaurants and seafood market. Handcrafted glassware shops on Sakaimachi Street. Canal boat cruises ~¥1,800.
  • Yoichi (1hr) — Nikka Whisky Distillery. Combine with Otaru for a full coastal day trip.
  • Sapporo (2–2.5hrs) — Ramen Yokocho (alley of 17 ramen shops), Susukino entertainment district, Sapporo Beer Museum (free entry, paid tastings). Shuttle bus ~¥2,500–6,600 one-way.
🍺

Après-Ski & Nightlife

Hirafu's nightlife is genuinely excellent — cosmopolitan, lively, and stays open late.

  • Bar Gyu+ — Iconic. Hidden fridge-door entrance, craft cocktails, good whisky. The starting point.
  • Bar Haku — Award-winning mixologist. Try the "Wa-jito" (Japanese mojito).
  • Tamashii — Wide spirits selection, billiard tables, live music and DJ sets. Food menu.
  • Powder Room — Niseko's premium nightclub. Lounge + dance floor. The late-night option.
  • Niseko Confidential — New bar from Hong Kong hospitality veterans. Creative cocktails and bar bites.

Money Tips

  • Japan is still largely a cash society in ski towns — bring yen or use 7-Eleven ATMs
  • Food in Japan is incredible value — a bowl of ramen is ~£4–6, a full izakaya dinner ~£15–25
  • Get a Wise or Revolut card for the best exchange rates
  • Convenience stores (konbini) are your friend — cheap, delicious food 24/7
  • Budget option: stay in a hostel + eat at konbini/ramen shops and you can do 2 weeks for ~£2,000
